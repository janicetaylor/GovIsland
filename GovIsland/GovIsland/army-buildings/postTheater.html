Post Theater (330)
Facing Owasco Road, north of Cartigan Road

Constructed in 1937, Building 330 is a two-story theater building on a concrete foundation faced with reddish-brown brick laid in American bond. The main block of the building has brick quoins at the corners and a slate-covered hipped roof. On the north side is a two-story gabled projection above the entrance portico. This projection has three blind arches with cast stone keystones and impost blocks, brick quoins, and a raking wood cornice outlining the gable. A projecting wing with a hipped roof has been added at the south end of the building.

This theater design, with a rectangular plan and neo-Georgian detail, was typical of those constructed on military bases during this period. It has continued to be used for the showing of films and as an auditorium.

In 1995, the theater was used for the annoucement that the Coast Guard was departing Governors Island.
