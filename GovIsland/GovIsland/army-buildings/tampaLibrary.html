Tampa Memorial Library (S-251)
Corner of Andes Road and Tampa Road

Building S-251 is a rectangular one-story wood frame structure set on a high brick basement. It was built about 1908 to serve the needs of the Fort Jay Quartermaster for storage and as a workshop. During World War I it was used for supplies and during World War II it was the post exchange (PX), selling everything from cigarettes to soap.

During the Coast Guard era, the structure became the post library and was named the Tampa Memorial Library, to commemorate the sinking of the USCGC Tampa. A German U-boat attacked and sunk the Tampa on Sept. 26, 1918, resulting in the highest U.S. combat casualty loss in World War I. The cutter sank with all hands, 115 officers and men, and 16 passengers.

Building S-251 served as the post library for many years. When the Coast Guard departed in 1997, the book-return box was left standing in front of the entrance. The short street west of the library was also named in honor of the Tampa. The building lies within the boundaries of the National Monument; the decision is up to the National Park Service to determine its fate.
