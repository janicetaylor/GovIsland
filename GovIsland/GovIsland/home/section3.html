About the Explorer's Guide
The Governors Island Explorer's Guide was created for visitors to the island to learn more about its history and to get the most out of a visit. The island only opened to the public in 2004 and there is very little information easily available about the history, amenities, and visitor information needed to enjoy a visit. The park is currently in transition as it enters the next phase of its development, so this guide was created to help visitors understand the 200 years of Governors Island history, as well as help those who just want to come out and enjoy a nice bike ride. The Governors Island Explorer's Guide was developed over five years of visits to the island, along with research into government publications, city history books, historic newspaper files, and institutional research.

Special thanks to the New-York Historical Society, New York Public Library, Museum of the City of New York, Brooklyn Museum, U.S. Library of Congress, New York Landmarks Preservation Commission, National Parks Service, and the Trust For Governors Island.

About Us

The New Amsterdam Apps & Maps Co. was founded in 2011 by Kevin C. Fitzpatrick and Janice Garingo. The mission of the company is to bring history to your fingertips in new and engaging ways.

Kevin C. Fitzpatrick
Writing, Research, Photography
Kevin C. Fitzpatrick is a licensed New York City Sightseeing Guide who has conducted city walking tours for 12 years. He is the author of "A Journey into Dorothy Parker's New York" and co-editor of "The Lost Algonquin Round Table." Fitzpatrick served in the U.S. Marine Corps from 1983-1989. He is a member of the National Genealogical Society and researches national cemeteries and World War I records. He has been president of the Dorothy Parker Society since 1999. Born in Baltimore, he resides on the Upper West Side and Shelter Island, NY. His favorite place on Governors Island is Brick Row Park during the Jazz Age Weekends.
