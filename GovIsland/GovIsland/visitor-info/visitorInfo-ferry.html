Visitor Info: Ferry
Ferry (Manhattan)
The free Governors Island ferry departs from the Battery Maritime Building located at 10 South Street, adjacent to the Staten Island Ferry in Lower Manhattan, on Fridays, Saturdays and Sundays.
Fridays:
Departures to Soissons Dock start at 10 AM and run every hour on the hour until 3 PM, which is the last boat. Departures to the Battery Maritime Building begin at 10:30 AM and run every hour on the half hour until 5:00 PM, which is the last boat.
Saturdays & Sundays:
Departures to Soissons Dock start at 10 AM and run every half hour on the hour after 11 AM until 5:30 PM, which is the last boat. Departures to the Battery Maritime Building begin at 10:30 AM and run every half hour after 11 AM until 7:00 PM, which is the last boat.

Manhattan NYHarborWay Ferry (Fridays ONLY) New York Water Taxi runs a ferry service from Manhattan to Brooklyn, with stops at Governors Island and Brooklyn Bridge Park. Fares are $2 for a single-ride ticket (one way) and $3 for a round-trip ticket, $6 for a family ride ticket (one way), which is good for up to two adults and two children over the age of three. Children fewer than three ride for free. Bicycles are allowed on the ferry.

Ferry (Brooklyn)
Free ferry service runs from Brooklyn to Governors Island every Saturday and Sunday. The ferry dock in Brooklyn is at the Fulton Ferry Landing, near the base of the Brooklyn Bridge. Departures are every 20 minutes starting at 11 AM. The last boat back to Brooklyn is at 7 PM. The free Brooklyn ferry does not stop in Manhattan. Bicycles are allowed on the ferry.

Ferry (New Jersey)
Commercial weekend ferry service to and from the Weehawken or Hoboken terminals makes stops at Pier 11 (Wall Street) in Manhattan, Fulton Ferry Landing in DUMBO, South Williamsburg at Schaefer Landing, North Williamsburg between North Fifth and North Sixth streets, Greenpoint at India Street, Long Island (Hunters Point South), and East 34th Street in Manhattan. Extra stops at Atlantic Avenue in Brooklyn (Brooklyn Bridge Park) and Governors Island will operate during summer weekends.

Fares will cost $3 or $5.50 per trip under a two-zone ticket system, with one zone meaning three stops or less, and two zones, four stops or more. Tickets will ultimately be available through the installation of ticket machines at many of the waterfront landings, but until those machines are operational, fares will be collected on board. Ferries will run approximately every 30 minutes during the summer. Hours of operation 9 a.m. to 7 p.m. on Saturday and Sunday. Bicycles are allowed on the ferry.
