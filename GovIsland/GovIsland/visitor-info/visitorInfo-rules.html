Island Rules and Regulations
All visitors and their packages are subject to search and seizure. The Trust for Governors Island reserves the right to deny access to the island at its discretion.

• Alcohol: Visitors cannot bring alcohol onto Governors Island. Water Taxi Beach has a bar and grill where alcohol can be purchased and consumed. Guards at the ferry terminal in Manhattan search bags.

• Bicycling: Leisure bicycling is permitted only in designated areas. Racing is not permitted. Speed limits are strictly enforced.

• Camping: No camping is permitted on the island.

• Cars: There is no public parking on Governors Island; the island is a car-free zone. There is a public parking garage across the street from the Battery Maritime Building on Whitehall Street.

• Children: All children 14 and under must be accompanies by an adult at all times.

• Controlled Substances: All controlled substances are banned on the island.

• Cooking/Grilling: Completely prohibited on the island.

• Docking Boats: Personal vessels are not permitted to dock on Governors Island. Kayaks are permitted to dock at Pier 101 only during island operating hours.

• Dogs: Pets are not permitted on Governors Island. Special needs dogs are permitted with proper paperwork and vest/collar.

• Firearms & Explosives: Leave your guns at home. Replica weapons for Civil War Reenactors require permission.

• Fishing: Anglers are allowed to drop their lines on the South Side of the island, on Buttermilk Channel. However, all fish caught must be put back; strict catch-and-release rules apply due to city and state law.

• Swimming: law permits no swimming in the harbor and off the piers.
