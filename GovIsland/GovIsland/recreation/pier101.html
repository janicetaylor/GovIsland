Pier 101
Next to the Battery Tunnel ventilation structure

Kayak to Governors Island? This is the place (on certain days; check the island's calendar). This is also the dock for ferries to Brooklyn and other smaller boats.

The passenger vessel landing dock Pier 101 was named San Juan Dock by the 16th Infantry Regiment in 1928, to commemorate the army's campaign in the Spanish-American War in 1898. There has been a dock at this point on Governors Island since at least 1800; stone steps remain from the 1820s underneath it. During World War II it was called the East Ferry slips.

Today it is the landing point for small commercial ferry service, including New York Water Taxi.
