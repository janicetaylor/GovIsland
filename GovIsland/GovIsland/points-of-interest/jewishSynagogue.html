Jewish Synagogue (S-40)
East of Barry Road, behind Bldg 8

The former Jewish Synagogue Shaare Shomayim (Hebrew for "Gates of Heaven") occupied Building S-40, a one-story rectangular frame structure built in 1918. The building is just off Barry Road, behind General's Row. The original wood siding was laid vertically; this has been replaced by horizontal clapboard. Building S-40 was originally constructed for temporary storage in 1918 as the Army was expanding its facilities on the island during World War I.

In the mid-20th century (perhaps the 1960s) it was converted for purposes of Jewish worship and as a meeting space. After the building was converted to a synagogue-the date is unknown-a Jewish star and Hebrew letters reading beit haknesset ("house of assembly") were affixed above the door; they are still in place. Stained-glass windows with nautical themes from the Book of Psalms grace the synagogue.

The Torah was removed when the Coast Guard left the base in 1996. The last rabbi on the island, William Kloner, a retired U.S. Navy Reserve captain from Brooklyn, took it away. The island's Torah had an interesting military history. Kloner told the Jewish Daily Forward in 2010 that he and the Torah had been at a chapel at Brooklyn's Floyd Bennett Field. Years earlier, a fire raged through the chapel that served the Jewish community on the airfield. A Navy pilot, a Presbyterian, rushed inside to rescue the scrolls. Today the Torah is at Fort Wadsworth, the Staten Island Coast Guard base.

Since Building S-40 is in the Historic District, it can't be removed, and it's future is uncertain.
